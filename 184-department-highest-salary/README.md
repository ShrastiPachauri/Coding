<p style="user-select: auto;">Write an SQL query to find employees who have the highest salary in each of the departments.</p>

<p style="user-select: auto;">Return the result table in <strong style="user-select: auto;">any order</strong>.</p>

<p style="user-select: auto;">The query result format is in the following example.</p>

<p style="user-select: auto;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="user-select: auto;"><strong style="user-select: auto;">Example 1:</strong></p>

<pre style="user-select: auto;"><strong style="user-select: auto;">Input:</strong> 
Employee table:
+----+-------+--------+--------------+
| id | name  | salary | departmentId |
+----+-------+--------+--------------+
| 1  | Joe   | 70000  | 1            |
| 2  | Jim   | 90000  | 1            |
| 3  | Henry | 80000  | 2            |
| 4  | Sam   | 60000  | 2            |
| 5  | Max   | 90000  | 1            |
+----+-------+--------+--------------+
Department table:
+----+-------+
| id | name  |
+----+-------+
| 1  | IT    |
| 2  | Sales |
+----+-------+
<strong style="user-select: auto;">Output:</strong> 
+------------+----------+--------+
| Department | Employee | Salary |
+------------+----------+--------+
| IT         | Jim      | 90000  |
| Sales      | Henry    | 80000  |
| IT         | Max      | 90000  |
+------------+----------+--------+
<strong style="user-select: auto;">Explanation:</strong> Max and Jim both have the highest salary in the IT department and Henry has the highest salary in the Sales department.
</pre>
